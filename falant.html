Falant Test (Fransworth Lantern Test)

The Farnsworth Lantern Test, or FALANT, is a test of color vision originally developed specifically to screen sailors for tasks requiring color vision, such as identifying signal lights at night. It has additionally been used to screen flying personnel.

Computer displays have only three primary colors. The colors of specially developed devices cannot be simulated by a display. The test can only determine (modarate and severe) red-green color blindness.
